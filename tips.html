TIPS

TIP #1: Burning DVDs

When you are burning DVDs always clean them before you burn them! There is nothing that ruins DVDs as easily as burning to them when they are dirty. Always clean them and avoid touching the printable area.

 

DVD ruined from fingerprint

TIP #2: DVD Storage

DVDs can be scratched easily and can make the DVD unusable if you are not carefull. The best way to prevent this is properly storing them. One of the best ways to store DVDs is by purchasing individual paper sleeves for your DVDs. When you are handling the DVDs with the paper sleevs on be sure to handle it by the rim of the DVD and not on the printable area. The thickness shouldn't be a problem unless you are storing them in a humid environment. You can purchase these from Amazon here. They are fairly thin so you might want to get others if you are storing your DVDs in a humid environment. If you do not have any DVD sleeves you can keep them in the spindle but it is not a tight fit so the DVDs could get scratched. I decided to expriment since I couldn't find much about this on the internet. I took a piece of paper and two DVDs. I applied the same force to each and here are the results.

The clear winner is the "scratch" printable area. I did this a few more times and the label side and paper all visibly had worse scratches. There isn't even a scratch there. Storing DVDs facing top to bottom is like storing your DVDs on really fine sandpaper and so is storing DVDs with paper dividing them so don't store them like that unless you want them to be scratched. Instead the best way to store your DVDs (without sleeves) is by storing them label to label, side to side.
